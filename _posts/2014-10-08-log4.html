My contribution to Wikipedia was a short sentence at the end of the Art Bergmann article. The new content details his latest album release. See figure 2, where the addition is circled.

My contribution did stick because the source was reputable and verifiable so the contribution would be difficult to contest. In addition, I am one of two subject matter experts on Bergmann's career.[1] As such, I am usually the first authority to review edits for accuracy.[2]

My impression of Wikipedia did not change after this exercise because I have made a few contributions in the past. However, after reading the New York Times article, Define Gender Gap? Look Up Wikipedia's Contributor List, I began to explore female bands/musicians of Bergmann's era. I discovered that few had articles; whereas, most, if not all, of the predominantly male bands/musicians were represented. For example, the Victoria all-female trio, The Dishrags, do not have an article even though they were invited by the London, UK band, The Clash, to open for them in Vancouver.

The overall Wikipedia process seems more the consensus of a few passionate editors than democratic. One feature is the Talk page associated with each article. Users may use this as an open forum to discuss contributions. Most often these discussions lead to consensus on a contentious edit.
